Creating a reading list of articles on opinion mining in the context of natural language processing (NLP), with a focus on the analysis of arguments from a social science perspective, involves selecting a mix of foundational and recent works. Here’s a curated list of 20 articles that cover these topics up to 2023:

1. **Pang, B., & Lee, L. (2008).** "Opinion mining and sentiment analysis." *Foundations and Trends in Information Retrieval*. This foundational paper provides a comprehensive overview of sentiment analysis techniques.

2. **Liu, B. (2012).** "Sentiment analysis and opinion mining." *Synthesis Lectures on Human Language Technologies*. A detailed introduction to the field of opinion mining.

3. **Cambria, E., Schuller, B., Xia, Y., & Havasi, C. (2013).** "New avenues in opinion mining and sentiment analysis." *IEEE Intelligent Systems*. Discusses emerging trends in sentiment analysis.

4. **Feldman, R. (2013).** "Techniques and applications for sentiment analysis." *Communications of the ACM*. Reviews various techniques used in sentiment analysis.

5. **Balahur, A., & Turchi, M. (2014).** "Comparative experiments using supervised learning and machine translation for multilingual sentiment analysis." *Computer Speech & Language*. Explores multilingual sentiment analysis.

6. **Ghosh, S., & Veale, T. (2016).** "Fracking sarcasm using neural network." *Proceedings of the 7th Workshop on Computational Approaches to Subjectivity, Sentiment and Social Media Analysis*. Focuses on detecting sarcasm in sentiment analysis.

7. **Ravi, K., & Ravi, V. (2015).** "A survey on opinion mining and sentiment analysis: Tasks, approaches and applications." *Knowledge-Based Systems*. Provides a survey of tasks and approaches in opinion mining.

8. **Zhang, L., Wang, S., & Liu, B. (2018).** "Deep learning for sentiment analysis: A survey." *Wiley Interdisciplinary Reviews: Data Mining and Knowledge Discovery*. Discusses the application of deep learning in sentiment analysis.

9. **Budzianowski, P., & Vulić, I. (2019).** "Hello, it’s GPT-2—how can I help you? Towards the use of pretrained language models for task-oriented dialogue systems." *Proceedings of the 3rd Workshop on Neural Generation and Translation*. Explores the use of language models in dialogue systems, relevant for opinion mining.

10. **Stab, C., & Gurevych, I. (2017).** "Parsing argumentation structures in persuasive essays." *Computational Linguistics*. Analyzes argumentation structures, bridging NLP and social sciences.

11. **Habernal, I., & Gurevych, I. (2017).** "Argumentation mining in user-generated web discourse." *Computational Linguistics*. Focuses on mining arguments from web discourse.

12. **Lawrence, J., & Reed, C. (2019).** "Argument mining: A survey." *Computational Linguistics*. Provides a comprehensive survey of argument mining techniques.

13. **Cabrio, E., & Villata, S. (2018).** "Five years of argument mining: A data-driven analysis." *Proceedings of the Twenty-Seventh International Joint Conference on Artificial Intelligence*. Reviews progress in argument mining.

14. **Bosc, T., & Cabrio, E. (2017).** "Tweeties squabbling: Positive and negative results in applying argument mining on social media." *Proceedings of the 2017 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing*. Examines argument mining on social media platforms.

15. **Al-Khatib, K., et al. (2016).** "Cross-domain mining of argumentative text through distant supervision." *Proceedings of the 15th Conference of the North American Chapter of the Association for Computational Linguistics: Human Language Technologies*. Discusses cross-domain argument mining.

16. **Reisert, P., et al. (2021).** "Argument mining from heterogeneous sources: A survey and challenges." *Artificial Intelligence Review*. Surveys argument mining across different sources.

17. **Lippi, M., & Torroni, P. (2016).** "Argument mining from speech: Detecting claims in political debates." *Proceedings of the Thirtieth AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence*. Focuses on argument mining in political debates.

18. **Wachsmuth, H., et al. (2017).** "Computational argumentation quality assessment in natural language." *Proceedings of the 15th Conference of the European Chapter of the Association for Computational Linguistics*. Discusses assessing the quality of arguments.

19. **Green, N. (2015).** "Identifying argumentation schemes in genetics research articles." *Proceedings of the 2nd Workshop on Argumentation Mining*. Applies argument mining to scientific texts.

20. **Daxenberger, J., et al. (2017).** "What is the essence of a claim? Cross-domain claim identification." *Proceedings of the 2017 Conference on Empirical Methods in Natural Language Processing*. Explores claim identification across domains.

These articles provide a broad overview of opinion mining and argument analysis, integrating perspectives from both NLP and social sciences.
